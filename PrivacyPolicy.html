Privacy Policy — ClaimVault

Last updated: 1 May 2026

ClaimVault ("we", "our", "the app") helps you keep track of your purchases, warranties, AMCs, insurance policies, and service schedules. This policy explains what data the app collects, why, and how we handle it.

1. Data we collect

Category What Why
Account info Email address, display name (from Google sign-in or magic-link signup) Authentication and to associate your data with your account
Equipment & coverage data Item names, brands, model numbers, serial numbers, purchase dates, prices, warranty/AMC/insurance details you enter or upload Core app functionality — this is the data the app exists to manage
Documents Receipts, invoices, warranty cards, AMC contracts, photos, PDFs you upload Storage and retrieval; OCR/text extraction for auto-fill
OpenAI API key Optional, encrypted at rest, only if you enable the AI Q&A feature Used only by your device to call OpenAI on your behalf
Device info (device_info_plus, package_info_plus) Device model, OS version, app version Crash diagnostics and support
Push notification token (firebase_messaging) Optional, only if you enable reminders Sending coverage-expiry reminders to your device

We do not collect: location, contacts, microphone audio, advertising identifiers, browsing history.

2. Permissions and what they're used for

Android permission What we use it for
INTERNET Sync data with our backend (Supabase) and call OpenAI when you use AI Q&A
CAMERA Take a photo of a receipt, warranty card, or document for upload
READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE / READ_MEDIA_IMAGES / READ_MEDIA_VIDEO Pick existing files (PDFs, CSVs, images) from your device to upload
POST_NOTIFICATIONS Show coverage-expiry and service-due reminders
WAKE_LOCK, RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED, VIBRATE Required by the notifications subsystem to deliver scheduled reminders

The camera, file picker, and notification permissions are only requested when you tap the corresponding feature.

3. Where data is stored

  • Database: Supabase (PostgreSQL). All rows are protected by row-level security; you can only read and write rows that belong to your user_id.
  • File storage: Supabase Storage, in a private bucket called documents. Files are scoped to your user folder.
  • OpenAI key: Encrypted on-device with a secret derived from your account, then stored on your users row in Supabase. Only the decrypted key is used in-memory at request time.
  • Local cache: Recent records may be cached in app-private storage by Flutter / Supabase SDK. Cache is cleared when you sign out or uninstall the app.

4. Third-party services

  • Supabase (data, auth, storage) — see https://supabase.com/privacy
  • Google Sign-In (auth provider) — only your email and display name are shared with us, and only when you choose to sign in
  • OpenAI — only if you supply your own API key and use the AI Q&A feature. Your question, equipment metadata, and document text are sent to OpenAI's chat completion endpoint. Refer to https://openai.com/policies/privacy-policy
  • Firebase Cloud Messaging — for push notifications, when enabled
  • Google ML Kit (on-device) — runs OCR locally on your device. Text never leaves the device for the OCR step.

5. Data sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your data. The only times your data leaves the app are:

  1. To and from Supabase (your own backend account, encrypted in transit via HTTPS)
  2. To OpenAI, only if you've enabled AI Q&A and supplied a key
  3. To Firebase, for push notifications you've opted in to

6. Your rights

You can:

  • Export all your data via the in-app Settings → Export
  • Delete your account and all associated data via Settings → Delete Account. This removes your users row, all equipment, documents, coverage plans, reminders, and Supabase Storage files via cascade.

For requests, contact: claimvault.support@example.com (replace with your real address before launch)

7. Children

ClaimVault is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect their data.

8. Security

  • All transport is HTTPS
  • Database uses row-level security (RLS) so users can only access their own rows
  • The OpenAI API key is encrypted at rest
  • Authentication tokens are stored in flutter_secure_storage (Android Keystore-backed)

9. Changes to this policy

We will update the date at the top of this document if the policy changes. Material changes will be announced in-app or via email.
